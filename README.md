## Background

## Assignments

### Assignment 1 — “Savings Analysis” Automation

The situation. This is our single biggest bottleneck. When a prospect is considering Source Club, we run a "savings analysis": we take the prospect's “purchase history” — an export of everything they currently buy from their dental supplier — and compare each line item against Source Club's negotiated pricing to show them their total potential savings. Today our founder does this by hand. It takes about 10 minutes per analysis, 20–40 times a month — 5 to 7+ hours a month of manual work — and it is the step we most want to automate.

The hard part is matching. The prospect's file and our pricing catalog rarely describe the same product the same way: different manufacturer SKUs, different descriptions, different pack sizes, different units of measure. A human can eyeball "is this the same box of gloves?" Software has to be taught to.

Here are videos and resources the team has given you to explain the process: [Savings Analysis Loom Links](https://www.notion.so/Savings-Analysis-Loom-Links-21405af7a9088244a90881a2323b0d93?pvs=21) 

Your task:

- Design the system that automates the savings analysis. How would you architect it end to end? What does the pipeline look like? Where does AI do the work, and where does a human stay in the loop? How do you handle the line items that don't cleanly match?
- If you can, build as much of a working proof-of-concept as you reasonably can in the time you have time. The more you can give us an idea of how this would work, the better.  Even working prototype on a slice of the data than a polished document describing one. Use whatever tools you would actually use on the job — Claude, spreadsheets, scripts, no-code tools, anything.
- Deliver: your architecture and approach, whatever you built (links or files), and a short note on what you would do next with more time.

Suggested time: 2–3 hours.

### Assignment 2 — Systems Architecture: Connecting Stripe and HubSpot

The situation. We bill through Stripe and run our CRM in HubSpot. One subscription in Stripe equals one dental practice location; a multi-location company is a single customer with several subscriptions. Right now the company and subscription names in Stripe don't match our HubSpot records — so nobody on the team can open a company in HubSpot and see its billing status without manually cross-referencing Stripe. We want subscription status, billing health, and revenue to show directly on the HubSpot company record.

Your task:

- Map at least two or three different ways this could be solved — for example a native integration, a middleware tool, a custom build. The options are your call.
- Pick one. Justify it: why this approach over the others, what it costs, and why it will be successful long term.

Suggested time: 30 min
